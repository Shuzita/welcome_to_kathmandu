About Kathmandu

Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, is a city that merges ancient traditions with the modern world. As the gateway to the Himalayas, it is known for its beautiful scenery and rich cultural history. Whether you are trekking in the surrounding mountains or exploring the narrow alleys of its bustling streets, Kathmandu offers something for every traveler. The city is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including temples and monuments that date back centuries.

Kathmandu is also a melting pot of cultures and religions, where Buddhists and Hindus live harmoniously. The vibrant festivals, the intricate wood carvings, and the colorful street markets are just a few of the things that make this city so unique.

Attractions

  • Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple)

    A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Swayambhunath offers panoramic views of Kathmandu.

  • Pashupatinath Temple

    One of the holiest Hindu temples, known for its beautiful architecture and sacred rituals.

  • Boudhanath Stupa

    The largest stupa in Nepal, a hub of Tibetan Buddhism with a rich history.

  • Durbar Square

    A historical plaza that showcases the art and architecture of the Malla kings.

  • Thamel Market

    A bustling area filled with shops, restaurants, and vibrant street life.
